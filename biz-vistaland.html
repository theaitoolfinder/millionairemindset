For Buyers and Investors

How OFWs Buy Property in the Philippines

You don't have to be physically present to own a home. Here's how the process works from anywhere in the world.

PAG-IBIG (HDMF) Financing

As an OFW, you contribute to PAG-IBIG. Those contributions allow you to borrow up to P6 million for a housing loan at competitive interest rates — typically 4.5–6.5% per year. Repayment terms of up to 30 years keep monthly payments low.

Bank Financing

Major Philippine banks — BDO, BPI, Metrobank, UnionBank — offer OFW housing loans. Your remittance records and employment contract serve as proof of income. Rates and terms vary; contact me for a side-by-side comparison.

Stop Paying Rent — Build Equity Instead

Every peso of rent is gone forever. A monthly mortgage payment, however, builds ownership. After 20–30 years, the house is fully yours — and worth far more than what you paid.

Property Appreciation in Key Areas

Cavite, Bulacan, Laguna, and key Visayas-Mindanao cities have shown consistent value growth driven by OFW demand and urban expansion. Buying today locks in today's price.
